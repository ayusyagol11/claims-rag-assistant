CLAIM #A2024-0112
-----------------
Worker: Female, 34, administrative officer, ACT government agency.
Incident: Repetitive strain injury to right wrist and forearm, diagnosed as de Quervain's
tenosynovitis. Onset gradual over 6 weeks of intensive keyboard and mouse use following
a team restructure that doubled her document processing workload. Formal injury reported
to supervisor on 14 March 2024, claim lodged 3 days post-reporting.
Medical: Initial GP assessment confirmed tendinopathy. Referred to hand physiotherapist
for 8 sessions. Anti-inflammatory medication prescribed. Ergonomic workstation assessment
conducted by Comcare-accredited OT in week 2 — adjustable keyboard tray and ergonomic
mouse recommended and installed by employer.
Return to Work: Modified duties commenced week 3 — verbal-only tasks, telephone liaison,
meeting facilitation. Graduated return to full keyboard duties over 6 weeks with 2-hourly
micro-break protocol. Worker returned to full pre-injury duties by week 10. No ongoing
restrictions at closure.
Liability and Reserve: Liability accepted under the Workers Compensation Act 1951 (ACT),
Section 30 (disease injuries). Mechanism consistent with employment conditions. Reserve
set at $9,200 covering physiotherapy, OT assessment, ergonomic equipment, and 3 weeks
of partial incapacity payments. Final payout $8,750. Claim closed at week 14.
Complexity flags: Nil. Employer cooperative, workplace modification straightforward.

CLAIM #A2024-0289
-----------------
Worker: Male, 52, construction labourer, private building contractor, Canberra suburb.
Incident: Acute lower back injury sustained while manually lifting a 35kg concrete block
without mechanical assistance. Heard audible pop at time of lift. Attended emergency
department same evening. MRI confirmed L4-L5 disc bulge with mild nerve root impingement.
Claim lodged 5 days post-incident.
Medical: Emergency department, GP follow-up, referred to spinal physiotherapist (12
sessions approved) and pain management specialist. Temporary prescription of muscle
relaxants and NSAIDs. Neurological symptoms (left leg tingling) resolved by week 6.
Return to Work: Initial period of total incapacity — 8 weeks. Graduated return to
modified duties week 9 (supervisory and site safety observer role, no manual handling).
Full manual handling duties cleared by treating GP at week 18 with a 20kg lifting
restriction remaining. Worker and employer agreed to permanent reassignment to leading
hand supervisory role. No further manual handling above 20kg post-closure.
Liability and Reserve: Liability accepted. Mechanism clearly consistent with employment.
Reserve set at $41,500 covering 8 weeks total incapacity payments (at applicable
weekly rate), 12 physiotherapy sessions, pain management consultation, specialist MRI,
and 10 weeks partial incapacity differential payments. Final payout $38,200.
Complexity flags: Employer initially disputed supervisory role classification and wage
differential. Resolved via conciliation at week 12. No solicitor involvement.

CLAIM #A2024-0334
-----------------
Worker: Female, 28, registered nurse, ACT private hospital.
Incident: Needle-stick injury sustained during IV cannulation procedure on a patient
with a complex blood-borne infection history. Injury occurred despite PPE use due to
unexpected patient movement. Incident reported immediately per hospital protocol.
Claim lodged same day.
Medical: Immediate post-exposure protocol initiated — wound management, baseline blood
tests, prophylactic antiretroviral medication commenced within 2 hours. Serial blood
tests at 6 weeks, 3 months, and 6 months. All results returned negative. Worker
experienced significant anxiety and insomnia during monitoring period — referred to
EAP psychologist (6 sessions).
Return to Work: No physical capacity restrictions — worker remained on full duties
throughout with psychological support. Modified roster to reduce high-acuity patient
contact during acute anxiety phase (weeks 2–5) at worker's request and treating
psychologist recommendation.
Liability and Reserve: Liability accepted as workplace injury. Reserve set at $7,800
covering post-exposure prophylaxis medication, serial blood tests, 6 psychology sessions,
and EAP coordination. Final payout $7,400. Claim closed at 6-month blood test mark
following confirmed negative result.
Complexity flags: Worker initially reluctant to engage EAP — 2 weeks of case manager
outreach required before referral accepted. Psychological support was critical to
maintaining workplace engagement throughout monitoring period.

CLAIM #A2024-0447
-----------------
Worker: Male, 61, school maintenance officer, ACT public school.
Incident: Fall from a 2.4-metre ladder while replacing a light fitting in a school
gymnasium. Left foot slipped from rung — worker fell backwards and landed on left
shoulder and elbow. Ambulance called. Hospital confirmed left proximal humerus fracture
(3-part) and radial head fracture. Surgery performed within 48 hours — ORIF (open
reduction internal fixation) of humerus. Claim lodged by employer on worker's behalf
on day 2 of hospitalisation.
Medical: 5-day hospital stay post-surgery. Discharge to home with sling immobilisation
for 6 weeks. Intensive physiotherapy commenced week 7 — range of motion, strength
rehabilitation over 22 sessions. Occupational therapy assessment at 3 months confirmed
significant residual restriction in overhead reach and load-bearing above 5kg.
Return to Work: Total incapacity for 14 weeks. Modified return — administrative and
grounds-level maintenance tasks (no ladders, no overhead work) from week 15. Given
worker's age and residual functional restriction, employer and case manager assessed
that return to full pre-injury ladder-based duties was unlikely. Vocational assessment
conducted at month 5 — identified modified maintenance role without height requirements
as permanent placement. Worker accepted and remains employed in this capacity.
Liability and Reserve: Liability accepted. Reserve set at $87,400 covering surgery and
hospitalisation gap, 14 weeks total incapacity payments, 22 physiotherapy sessions, OT
assessment, vocational assessment, and ongoing partial incapacity differential. Final
payout ongoing at claim review — projected $81,000 to closure.
Complexity flags: High complexity. Surgical case, significant residual impairment, age
flag for recovery trajectory. Employer required significant support to identify suitable
modified role. Claim remains open at 12-month mark for ongoing partial incapacity
monitoring and potential impairment assessment referral.

CLAIM #A2024-0521
-----------------
Worker: Female, 45, warehouse pick-and-pack operator, logistics company, ACT.
Incident: Slip on wet floor in refrigerated storage area — wet floor sign had not been
placed following a refrigeration unit leak. Worker fell forward, sustaining a left knee
injury — patella contusion and anterior cruciate ligament (ACL) partial tear confirmed
on MRI. Claim lodged 2 days post-incident.
Medical: Immobilisation, GP referral, orthopaedic specialist review at week 2.
Conservative management recommended over immediate surgery given partial tear grade.
Physiotherapy — 16 sessions over 12 weeks. Hydrotherapy added at week 8. Specialist
review at 3 months confirmed satisfactory healing without surgical intervention required.
Return to Work: Total incapacity 6 weeks. Modified duties from week 7 — seated quality
control and packaging inspection role (no walking on uneven surfaces, no kneeling,
no load-bearing above 10kg). Graduated return to full warehouse duties by week 20 with
permanent restriction of working in refrigerated areas (employer complied — warm
warehouse zone reassignment).
Liability and Reserve: Liability accepted with note of employer contributory negligence
(wet floor sign not deployed — WorkSafe ACT notified separately). Reserve set at
$28,500 covering 6 weeks total incapacity, 16 physio sessions, hydrotherapy, specialist
consultations, and 14 weeks partial incapacity differential. Final payout $26,100.
Complexity flags: WorkSafe ACT investigation conducted concurrently — employer issued
improvement notice. No impact on claim liability or payments.

CLAIM #A2024-0603
-----------------
Worker: Male, 39, IT systems technician, federal government agency, Canberra CBD.
Incident: Psychological injury — diagnosed adjustment disorder with anxiety, attributed
to a sustained period of workplace bullying by a direct line manager over 8 months.
Worker documented 14 specific incidents including public humiliation, exclusion from
meetings, and removal of project responsibilities without explanation. Formal complaint
lodged with HR prior to claim — HR investigation found substantiated bullying in 3 of
14 incidents. Claim lodged following treating psychiatrist diagnosis.
Medical: GP referral to psychiatrist — diagnosis confirmed at week 2. Commenced
weekly psychology sessions (CBT-based). Short-term SSRI medication prescribed.
Capacity assessment: unfit for work in current role and workplace for 12 weeks.
Return to Work: Worker unable to return to original team — employer arranged temporary
placement in separate directorate under a different reporting line for weeks 13–20.
Graduated psychological capacity improvement documented by treating psychologist.
Worker accepted voluntary internal transfer at month 6 to different branch — no
ongoing psychological incapacity at transfer date.
Liability and Reserve: Liability accepted for substantiated component of claim. Reserve
set at $34,200 covering 12 weeks total incapacity, psychiatrist and psychologist
sessions (36 total approved), medication, and workplace rehabilitation coordination.
Final payout $31,800. Claim closed at month 9.
Complexity flags: Very high complexity. Manager placed on administrative review during
claim. Employer relations strained — required significant case manager mediation.
Legal representation by worker from week 4 — all communications via solicitor.
Claim required senior case manager oversight from week 6 onward.

CLAIM #A2024-0718
-----------------
Worker: Female, 23, hospitality worker (barista), Canberra inner-north cafe.
Incident: Burn injury to right forearm and hand — steam wand from commercial espresso
machine contacted skin during a busy morning service period. Equipment had a known
defective steam valve that had been flagged to management 3 weeks prior without action.
Burn classified as partial-thickness (second degree) across 4% body surface area.
Claim lodged same day.
Medical: Hospital emergency — wound cleaned, non-adherent dressings applied. Referred
to specialist burns clinic for 6 follow-up appointments. Wound healing confirmed at
week 8 with minimal scarring. No skin grafting required. No occupational therapy needed.
Return to Work: Total incapacity for 3 weeks (wound dressing management, pain, heat
exposure risk). Modified return week 4 — front-of-house, no equipment operation.
Full return to barista duties week 8 following burns clinic discharge.
Liability and Reserve: Liability accepted. Equipment defect documented — WorkSafe ACT
notified of prior management awareness. Reserve set at $6,300 covering 3 weeks total
incapacity, 6 burns clinic appointments, and dressing supplies. Final payout $5,900.
Complexity flags: Employer issued prohibition notice by WorkSafe ACT for defective
equipment — machine repaired prior to worker's return. Worker did not pursue common
law claim despite solicitor approach at week 2.

CLAIM #A2024-0834
-----------------
Worker: Male, 57, bus driver, ACT public transport operator.
Incident: Aggravation of pre-existing lumbar spondylosis attributed to prolonged
vibration exposure over 18 years of bus driving. Worker reported worsening lower back
pain over 3-month period — treating GP noted significant deterioration compared to
prior imaging from 2019. GP opinion that occupational vibration was a substantial
contributing factor to acute-on-chronic exacerbation. Claim lodged under disease
injury provisions.
Medical: GP, physiotherapy (10 sessions), specialist spinal physician review. Spinal
physician confirmed occupational vibration as contributing factor consistent with
literature. CT scan showed progression of spondylosis beyond age-expected norms.
Ongoing pain management regime established. Opioid medication avoided per specialist
preference — TENS machine and heat therapy protocol.
Return to Work: Worker certified unfit for bus driving duties for 8 weeks. Offered
customer service and depot logistics role during recovery — worker accepted. Cleared
for gradual return to driving duties at week 9 with a maximum 4-hour driving shift
limit (employer approved through rostering adjustment). Permanent 4-hour driving
limit remains at claim closure.
Liability and Reserve: Liability accepted under Section 30 (disease injury — employment
a significant contributing factor). Pre-existing condition contribution noted in file
but did not reduce liability. Reserve set at $19,800 covering 8 weeks total incapacity,
physiotherapy, specialist consultation, CT imaging, and 10 weeks partial incapacity
differential. Final payout $18,400.
Complexity flags: Apportionment discussion between insurer and employer regarding
pre-existing condition contribution — resolved in favour of full acceptance per ACT
Act provisions. Worker's union involved from week 1 — cooperative engagement overall.

CLAIM #A2024-0912
-----------------
Worker: Female, 31, early childhood educator, private childcare centre, Canberra south.
Incident: Hearing damage — noise-induced hearing loss identified during routine
audiometric testing. Worker reported progressive difficulty hearing high-frequency
sounds over 12 months. Audiologist confirmed bilateral high-frequency sensorineural
hearing loss pattern consistent with chronic noise exposure. Childcare environment
noise levels measured at 82–88 dB during peak periods — above recommended 8-hour
exposure limit per Safe Work Australia guidelines. Claim lodged following audiologist
report.
Medical: Audiologist assessment confirmed occupational hearing loss. Referred to
ENT specialist — cochlear damage confirmed as permanent. Hearing aids prescribed and
fitted (bilateral, high-frequency amplification). No improvement expected with treatment
— condition is permanent.
Return to Work: No capacity restrictions — worker remained on full duties throughout.
Employer required to implement noise management controls (acoustic ceiling panels,
quiet room rotation policy, mandatory hearing protection during outdoor play).
Liability and Reserve: Liability accepted under disease injury provisions. Reserve set
at $14,100 covering audiologist assessment, ENT specialist, bilateral hearing aids
(initial pair), and one scheduled replacement pair. Ongoing monitoring reserve for
future audiometric reviews. Final payout $13,200.
Complexity flags: Employer initially disputed noise levels — independent hygienist
measurement engaged by insurer confirmed readings. Permanent impairment referral
lodged — impairment assessment pending at claim review date.

CLAIM #A2024-1047
-----------------
Worker: Male, 42, warehouse operator, logistics company, Canberra airport precinct.
Incident: Slip on wet floor in loading bay area — floor wet following roof leak during
heavy rainfall event. Wet floor warning sign was not placed. Worker fell and sustained
lower back muscular strain and right wrist sprain (no fractures confirmed on X-ray).
Attended GP same day. Claim lodged 3 days post-incident.
Medical: GP assessment, X-ray (wrist and lumbar — no fractures), referred to
physiotherapist. 8 physiotherapy sessions approved. Anti-inflammatory medication
prescribed short-term. Wrist splint worn for 2 weeks. Lumbar strain resolved at
week 5. Wrist assessed as fully functional at week 6.
Return to Work: Modified duties commenced week 2 — light picking duties, no repetitive
wrist flexion, no lifting above 5kg. Full duties cleared by treating GP at week 6.
Worker returned to full pre-injury role with no restrictions.
Liability and Reserve: Liability accepted. Reserve set at $18,500 covering 1 week
total incapacity, 5 weeks partial incapacity differential payments, 8 physiotherapy
sessions, GP consultations, and imaging. Final payout $17,200. Claim closed at week 8.
Complexity flags: Nil. Straightforward soft-tissue claim. Employer cooperative and
workplace modification straightforward. Wet floor sign protocol reviewed by employer
post-incident.
